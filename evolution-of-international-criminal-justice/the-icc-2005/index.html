2005

January 7, 2005: The situation in the Central African Republic is referred to the ICC. After conducting a preliminary investigation, the Office of the Prosecutor opened a formal investigation on May 22, 2007.

sources: “Central African Republic, ICC-01/05, http://www.icc-cpi.int/en_menus/icc/situations%20and%20cases/situations/situation%20icc%200105/Pages/situation%20icc-0105.aspx; Press Release: Prosecutor opens investigation in the Central African Republic, ICC-OTP-20070522-220, May 22, 2007, http://www.icc-cpi.int/en_menus/icc/press%20and%20media/press%20releases/2007/Pages/prosecutor%20opens%20investigation%20in%20the%20central%20african%20republic.aspx.”

2005

March 31, 2005: The Security Council referred the situation in Darfur, Republic of the Sudan, to the ICC through Resolution 1593. The situation in the Republic of the Sudan was the first Security Council referral to the Court. After conducting a preliminary investigation, the Office of the Prosecutor opened a formal investigation on July 6, 2005.

sources: “S/RES/1593 (March 31, 2005), http://www.icc-cpi.int/NR/rdonlyres/85FEBD1A-29F8-4EC4-9566-48EDF55CC587/283244/N0529273.pdf; Darfur, Sudan, ICC-02/05, http://www.icc-cpi.int/en_menus/icc/situations%20and%20cases/situations/situation%20icc%200205/Pages/situation%20icc-0205.aspx; Press Release: The Prosecutor of the ICC opens investigation in Darfur, ICC-OTP-066-104, July 6, 2005, http://www.icc-cpi.int/en_menus/icc/press%20and%20media/press%20releases/2005/Pages/the%20prosecutor%20of%20the%20icc%20opens%20investigation%20in%20darfur.aspx.”
